About Me

In the Cleveland area, soprano Jessica Fain has appeared as a soloist with the Carter Woods Chorale, the R. Nathaniel Dett Choir of the Cleveland School of the Arts, and the Adult Vocal Jazz Ensemble at the Cleveland Music Settlement. Ms. Fain has also been featured with the award winning Jeremy Winston Chorale during recent European tours with the Oscar winning Czech National Symphony Orchestra, and will be featured during upcoming tours this fall and next summer.

As a chorister, Jessica Fain has performed with the Choirs of Shaker Heights High School as a soloist abroad in the cities of Venice, Florence, Orvieto, and Rome. She was a member of the Kentucky State University Concert Choir, Kentucky State’s Opera Music Theater Workshop, Madrigal Ensemble, as well as the Kentucky State University Gospel Ensemble. Ms. Fain has performed in Severance Hall, the Cleveland Institute of Music, The Cleveland Music Settlement, St. Mark’s Basilica in Venice, St. Peter’s Basilica in Vatican City, Rome, Metropolitan Cathedral of the Assumption of Mary of Mexico City, The Smetena Hall in Prague, as well as churches across Germany.

A native of Cleveland Ohio, Jessica is currently studying voice with Willie Dycks. Ms. Fain received her Bachelor’s degree in music from Kentucky State University, with departmental honors in Voice. Ms. Fain’s recent projects included a solo recital at Antioch Baptist Church, solo appearances with the Carter Woods Chorale, as well as a chorister with the Cleveland Orchestra’s Blossom Festival Chorus in the summer of 2014. She also is a touring vocalist with the Jeremy Winston Chorale which was founded in 2012. The Jeremy Winston Chorale has made an indelible mark in the music community. The Chorale’s inaugural performance was at the world’s largest choral competition, The World Choir Games in Cincinnati, Ohio. Competing against world-class choirs from around the globe, The Jeremy Winston Chorale was crowned as gold medal champions as Best Gospel Choir, and silver medals for Mixed Chorus (classical music) and Spirituals.

Since winning at The World Choir Games, The Chorale has been featured in many outstanding performances including a performance for President Barack Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama at The White House in December of 2012 and an encore performance in December of 2015. The Chorus was featured as guests performers with The Czech National Symphony in their Prague Proms concert series during the summers of 2015 and 2016. with the CNSO again during their PROMS concert series during their 2016 concert season. The chorale also had the most viewed video in Germany in 2016, with over 4 million views from their first Flash Mob at the Europa Passage in Hamburg.
